## Executive Summary

WF1 (Content) and WF2 (Outreach) are the **trust layer**. Without them, the flywheel can generate money but not compound trust — and trust is what makes WF3/WF4 products sell at a premium.

The trap: both workflows can expand infinitely. This doc defines the **minimum viable cadence** — enough to keep the trust engine warm without consuming Paijo's bandwidth.

### Rule: 1-2 hours per week total. Automate everything else.

## The Minimal Viable Content Flywheel

This is the smallest loop that actually works:

```
1. Post educational content (1ai-content auto-generates, Paijo batch-reviews)
   ↓
2. Proof posts (trading screenshots, deal results — auto-pulled from 1ai-trade-cex + marketplace)
   ↓
3. Audience sees proof + education → follows + engages
   ↓
4. CTA in bio + posts → leads try Vilona TradeFX (WF4)
   ↓
5. Converts to subscriber → revenue → Capital Pool
   ↓
6. More capital → more product development → better proof → back to step 2
```

**What makes it "minimal viable":** Steps 1 + 2 are 90% automated. Paijo only reviews, doesn't create. But the loop still works because the automation produces real, specific content (not generic AI slop).

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ **Posting daily without a system** — leads to burnout and inconsistency  
❌ **Generic AI content** ("Here are 5 tips about trading!") — zero differentiation  
❌ **Outreach without personalization** — gets ignored or damages reputation  
❌ **Metrics obsession too early** — Month 1 is learning, not optimizing  
❌ **Trying all platforms simultaneously** — pick 2-3 and go deep first (TikTok + Telegram)

## Conclusion

WF1 + WF2 don't need complex infra in Month 1. They need:
1. **Content templates** (create once, reuse forever)
2. **Automation backbone** (PostBridge already exists — just use it)
3. **Consistent weekly rhythm** (50 min/week, no exceptions)
4. **Proof-first content strategy** (let real results do the selling)

The trust flywheel starts turning the moment the first piece of real proof (trading result, arbitrage deal, subscriber milestone) goes live. Everything else follows.
